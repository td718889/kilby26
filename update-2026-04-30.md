# KBP26 Guide Update — April 30

Two weeks out. The site is basically a full-blown festival planning app at this point — search, stage filters, a rating editor, localStorage autosave, a literal "Publish to Site" button that deploys to Netlify. Taylor built more features into this thing than some startups ship in a quarter.

The guide has takes on roughly 40 artists across all three days. The 5/5 tier is locked in: **Blood Orange** (his #16 all-time artist — "Champagne Coast, You're Not Good Enough, Jewelry, Smoke"), **Snail Mail** ("I love this sad little vulnerable Lesbian"), **Modest Mouse** (#39 all-time, 283 scrobbles — and there's a new album dropping June 5, which means the setlist could be spicy), **The xx**, and **Lorde** closing out Sunday. That's a murderers' row of a headliner run.

The hot conflict on Saturday: Drugdealer vs. Snail Mail at 4:50. His take on Drugdealer: *"'Pictures of You' is excellent. This is actually a great band and a tough time slot. But I probably gonna go with my sad, vulnerable lesbian Snail Mail."* So. That's settled.

Some other highlights from the takes file: Die Spitz gets a flat "Nope - too much noise. Im too old." Father John Misty is "edging towards being overplayed (in SLC and in general)." And local boy Ritt Momney apparently has 1.2M monthly Spotify listeners now — "TBH I had no idea. I assumed these guys were still playing tiny venues around the valley."

The xx gets a minor asterisk: "I'd rather it be just Jamie xx" — but still a 5/5, so the vibes win.

Artist summaries got a full rewrite this week — cleaner, more neutral bios with real critical comparisons. Still chipping away at the Sunday deep cuts. More takes incoming before May 15.
